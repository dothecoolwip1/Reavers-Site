About the sport

What is Buhurt and armored combat

Buhurt is a style of full contact armored fighting where teams in steel armor fight inside a fenced list. The goal is to put the other side on the ground using strikes, grappling, and teamwork.

It looks intense and it is, but it runs on strict rules, equipment checks, and a culture of respect.

  • Steel armor and helmets built for heavy contact.
  • Blunted weapons inspected before every event.
  • Rounds end when most of one team is off their feet.

Feels like

Rugby, grappling, and strongman combined with metal armor and a fence around you.

Values

Hard hits, clean sportsmanship, and going for a burger with your opponents later.

What a typical week looks like

Exact days change with seasons and space rentals, but this gives you an idea of how we balance strength, skills, and armor.

Day Focus Notes
Midweek Reavers Crucible strength and cardio Carries, pushes, odd objects, conditioning.
Weekend Soft kit skills and armor time Footwork, clinch, list games, steel for people with kits.
Extras Optional small group work Film review, armor checks, or extra cardio when folks are keen.

We adjust times when people work shift, have kids activities, or events on the road. The idea is to keep things realistic for normal life.

First night

What happens when you show up

  1. We say hello and go over a short waiver and basic rules.
  2. You join the warm up with everyone at your own pace.
  3. We pair you with someone experienced for simple movement drills.
  4. If you enjoy it, you keep coming and we slowly add more contact.

You are never thrown straight into a full metal brawl. We build you up so your body and brain can keep up and you can still go to work the next day.

Armor path

How we handle gear and money

Steel armor can be expensive, so we do not expect anyone to buy a full kit right away. The plan is to move in stages that match your budget and interest.

  • Stage one: soft kit, loaner shields, and simple padded weapons.
  • Stage two: starter armor like helmet, gauntlets, and good gloves.
  • Stage three: full kit built over time, tuned to your body and position.

We can point you at trusted armor makers and explain what is worth spending on first so you do not waste money.

Safety first

  • Armor and weapons are checked before you use them in contact.
  • Neck, hands, and head protection are always taken seriously.
  • If something feels wrong you can always step out and reset.
