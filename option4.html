The sport

Full contact armored combat with teams in steel armor, swinging blunted weapons inside a fenced list.

  • Steel armor and helmets checked before fights.
  • Blunted metal weapons with clear rules.
  • Rounds end when one side is mostly down.

Community

A mix of beginners and experienced fighters who want hard training and a crew that has their back.

What is Buhurt

Buhurt is a full contact sport using historically inspired armor with modern safety rules.

  • Team fights where last side standing wins.
  • Different formats like three person, five person, and larger melees.
  • Heavy grappling, trips, and pressure at the rail.

Who we train

We train people with all kinds of backgrounds and fitness levels.

  • People who never played contact sports before.
  • People with martial arts or combat sports experience.
  • Parents scheduling training around kids and work.

How we keep it safe

Safety checks and training habits matter more than winning any single fight.

  • Armor and weapons checked often.
  • Rules enforced by trained marshals.
  • Focus on control and calling it early when needed.

Practice structure

A typical block of training mixes movement, strength, and list skills. New people and armored fighters share the space with appropriate roles.

Segment Focus Notes
Warm up Mobility and light cardio Get joints warm and test how your body feels.
Strength Carries and pushes Odd objects that feel like armor weight.
Skills Footwork and clinch Soft kit drills and controlled contact.
Armor block List rounds in steel For people with full kits and clearance.

First time at practice

You can stop and watch any time, sit a round out if you feel off, and ask questions before you try anything new.
