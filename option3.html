What it is like to join the Red Deer Reavers

This page reads like a long answer to the question people ask most. What am I getting myself into if I come out to practice

What we actually do on the list

Red Deer Reavers trains for Buhurt, a form of full contact armored combat. Fighters wear full steel armor, use blunted weapons, and fight in teams inside a fenced list. The goal is to put the other side on the ground while staying on your own feet.

It is loud, heavy, and tiring, but it is not chaos. There are rules, experienced marshals, and a lot of respect between fighters.

  • Armor and weapons are checked before they enter the list.
  • There are clear illegal targets and actions that are not allowed.
  • Medical trained people are present at events.

Who shows up to train

People who train with us come from all kinds of backgrounds. Some have done judo, rugby, or lifting. Others have never played a contact sport before.

The common thread is that everyone wants a serious challenge and a team to belong to, but they still have jobs, kids, and busy lives.

  • Adults and older teens who want something different from a regular gym.
  • Parents who schedule training around family life.
  • People who want to compete or people who just love training in armor.

What your first night looks like

A lot of people worry they will walk into the gym and be thrown straight into a pile of steel. That is not how we run things.

Your first night is about learning how we move and how we keep things safe.

  1. We greet you, go over a short waiver, and talk through basic safety.
  2. You join the warm up at your own pace.
  3. We pair you with someone experienced for simple movement and balance drills.
  4. If you enjoy it, you keep coming and we slowly add more contact over time.

How training is split across the week

The exact days move around with seasons and space, but the structure stays similar. One session is more strength and conditioning, and one is more skills and armor time.

Session Focus Examples
Reavers Crucible Strength and conditioning Carries, drags, pushes, sprints, and grip work.
Skills and list work Footwork, clinch, list games Soft kit drills and armor work for people with gear.

How we handle gear and cost

A full set of armor can be a big investment. We do not expect new people to show up with a full kit or buy everything at once.

Instead we think in stages and help you make smart choices so you are not buying things twice.

  • Stage one is soft kit and loaner shields or padded weapons.
  • Stage two adds key protection like helmet, neck, and hands.
  • Stage three is a full kit built piece by piece as you are ready.

What about injuries and safety

Any full contact sport carries some risk, and we are honest about that. At the same time we do everything we can to keep those risks as low as possible.

  • We make sure armor fits properly before it sees contact.
  • We teach safe falling, posting, and how to tap out if something feels off.
  • We avoid people fighting when they are too tired to protect themselves.

How to actually get started

If you have read this far and the idea still sounds good, the next step is simple.

  1. Send us a message on Facebook, Instagram, or Discord.
  2. Tell us a little about your schedule and any injuries we should know about.
  3. We point you at a practice date and tell you exactly what to bring.
